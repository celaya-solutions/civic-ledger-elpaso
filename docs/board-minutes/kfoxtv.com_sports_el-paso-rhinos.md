Upcoming El Paso Rhinos season cancelled
The most decorated team in the city will not be returning to action in the near future. In a statement released Tuesday night, theWestern States Hockey League announced they will be cancelling the 2020-21 season. The announcement comes after the 2020 playoffs were cancelled back in March due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The El Paso Rhinos, the leagues defending Thorne Cup Champions, have yet to comment on the leagues decision but said a statement should be released in the coming days.

El Paso Rhinos find new home with NAHL
Weeks after the El Paso Rhinos announced they would be competing in the Fall despite not being associated with a league, the North American Hockey League (NAHL) announced that its Board of Governors approved the membership application for the Rhinos. The El Paso Rhinos will compete in the South Division beginning in the 2021-22 season and play out of the El Paso County Events Center. The team will also join the North American 3 Hockey League (NA3HL) for the 2020-21 season.
