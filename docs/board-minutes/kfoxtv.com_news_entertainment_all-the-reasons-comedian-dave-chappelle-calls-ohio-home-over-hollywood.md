All the reasons Dave Chappelle calls Ohio home over Hollywood, in his own words
Dave Chappelle may be one of the most recognizable stand-up comedians in the country, but away from the stage he’s chosen a quieter life far from Hollywood. That life is rooted in Ohio, specifically Yellow Springs, a small town that has become central to both his personal story and his public identity.
Dave Chappelle’s Childhood Ties to Ohio Explained
Chappelle’s connection to Ohio dates back to childhood. After his parents divorced, he spent most of the year in Maryland with his mother, but summers with his father in Yellow Springs. Those early memories stayed with him long after fame arrived.
During a 2017 appearance on “The Late Show with Stephen Colbert,” he described Yellow Springs, population roughly 3,700, as a hippie town where crystals and tie-dye are common and called it “a Bernie Sanders island in a [Donald] Trump sea.”
“Everybody knows me. It’s like The Simpsons, you see the same people every day, and I’m like the local ‘Krusty the clown,’” Chappelle joked.

Why Dave Chappelle Chose Ohio Over Hollywood
After walking away from “Chappelle’s Show” and a reported $50 million Comedy Central deal in 2005, the comedian returned to Ohio full-time. He now lives on a 39-acre farm with his wife, Elaine, and their three children, Sulayman, Ibrahim, and Sanaa. Chappelle has said the move gave his family a sense of normalcy he couldn’t find elsewhere.
In a 2017 interview with Gayle King on “CBS This Morning,” he compared his life in Ohio to a sanctuary. “It’s like a nature reserve where they let me roam free. I’m a protected commodity here,” he said.

From Netflix Specials to Town Halls: Chappelle’s Ohio Impact
Ohio frequently finds its way into Chappelle’s comedy as well. In several Netflix specials, he jokes about growing up as a minority around “poor white people” in Ohio and later addresses the state’s drug crisis in “Dave Chappelle: Sticks & Stones.”
His affection for the state was on full display during his 2018 episode of “My Next Guest Needs No Introduction with David Letterman,” where he toured Yellow Springs and reflected on its unchanged character.
“This place hasn’t changed in 100 years. It’s aesthetically almost identical. I remember walking and getting groceries here with my dad,” Chappelle told Letterman. He also shared local lore about the town’s spring, saying, “The local legend is, if you drink from the spring, you’ll always come back to this place.”
“Have you ever drank anything out of the spring?” Letterman asked.
“Clearly, I have drank a lot,” Chappelle replied.
How Dave Chappelle Supports and Invests in His Ohio Community
Beyond nostalgia, Chappelle has taken an active role in local affairs. He has spoken at town hall meetings, addressed noise complaints tied to his outdoor comedy shows, and highlighted the economic impact of hosting events in a nearby cornfield, shows that reportedly generated $12 million for Ohio, including $4 million for Yellow Springs.
“My town was dying ... I did shows in my neighbor’s cornfield, and these shows were very successful and may have even helped save the town,” he recalled during a 2020 “Saturday Night Live” monologue.
Whether through comedy, community involvement, or commentary on national politics, Chappelle’s loyalty to Ohio remains constant. As he once joked, Yellow Springs “has the most beautiful women in the world ... but it all depends on what you’re into.”
