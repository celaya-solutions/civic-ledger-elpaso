# Comparable Jurisdictions — Data Center Utility Controls

## Loudoun County, Virginia

**Approach:** Data center infrastructure escrows + grid upgrade cost allocation
**Key control:** Developers fund electrical grid upgrades proportional to load
**Outcome:** ~$100M in developer-funded infrastructure (2018-2024)

**Source:** [Loudoun County Board of Supervisors records]

## Mesa, Arizona

**Approach:** Water supply agreements with performance guarantees
**Key control:** Data centers must demonstrate water source + conservation plans
**Outcome:** Approved 3 data centers with verified water supply (2020-2023)

**Source:** [Mesa City Council minutes]

## Raleigh-Durham, North Carolina

**Approach:** Utility rate structure transparency + peak demand surcharges
**Key control:** Public disclosure of large customer rate schedules
**Outcome:** Resident coalition used data to negotiate rate reform (2019)

**Source:** [NC Utilities Commission public records]

## Analysis

**Common elements across all jurisdictions:**
1. ✅ Proof requirements (don't trust, verify)
2. ✅ Developer-funded infrastructure (no ratepayer subsidy)
3. ✅ Public disclosure of terms
4. ✅ Enforcement tied to permits/service

**None of these jurisdictions:**
- ❌ Blocked data center development
- ❌ Made requirements retroactive
- ❌ Required real-time public meter access

**Implication for El Paso:** Proposed controls are WITHIN industry norms.
