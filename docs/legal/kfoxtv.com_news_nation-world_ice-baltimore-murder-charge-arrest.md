Suspect in murder case held by ICE in Baltimore
MARYLAND (WBFF) — ICE Baltimore announced the arrest of an undocumented man from Guyana who is facing a murder charge on Friday.
Officials identified the suspect as Christopher Adolph Franklin, who has been convicted of first-degree murder and the felony use of a handgun.
The arrest was posted on ICE Baltimore’s social media on January 2.
Franklin arrived in the United States in 1985 as a lawful permanent resident but violated the terms of his status when he committed murder in Maryland in 1999, according to authorities.
Franklin is currently in ICE custody as he awaits his removal from the country.
