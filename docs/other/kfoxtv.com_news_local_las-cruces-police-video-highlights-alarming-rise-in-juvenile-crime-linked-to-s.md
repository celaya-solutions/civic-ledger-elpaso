Las Cruces police video highlights alarming rise in juvenile crime linked to social media
LAS CRUCES, NEW MEXICO (KFOX14/CBS4) â€” A newly released video by the Las Cruces Police Department is drawing attention to the troubling rise in juvenile crime, particularly involving firearms and social media.
The video, part of the "Bridging the Badge" initiative, showcases several juveniles firing guns in public spaces, underscoring the department's concerns as the new year begins.

Police Chief Jeremy Story emphasized the growing issue, stating, "There's been far too many crimes involving juveniles."
He noted that many juveniles engage in dangerous activities for social media attention, saying, "A lot of what they're doing is for the 'Gram'. It's for the likes."
The video also presented data from 2020 to the present, revealing:
- Out of 83 juveniles, five to nine have been arrested
- 41% of these crimes being felonies
- 17 juveniles were charged with murder or manslaughter
- 128 with aggravated battery or assault
- 217 with weapons violations
Residents expressed concern about the safety of public spaces, with one saying, "It just kind of makes it to where it's scary when we want to come to a place like this, a park to bring our kids."
Another resident supported the police's focus on juvenile crime, stating, "I think it's a valiant effort. I think it's worthwhile."
